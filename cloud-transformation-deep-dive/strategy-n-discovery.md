# Now let’s make this real

You are now in:

## 🎯 Your First Steering Committee Meeting

Participants:

*   CIO
*   CFO
*   CISO
*   Head of Infrastructure
*   Head of Applications
*   Accenture Program Director (looking at you)
*   You (Technical Owner)

* * *

## Situation

CIO says:

> “We want aggressive cloud adoption and modernization.”

CFO says:

> “We expect cost savings within 12–18 months.”

CISO says:

> “No compromise on compliance and data security.”

Infra Head says:

> “Our current environment is already unstable.”

* * *

### Your move

You cannot agree with everyone blindly. 
You need to respond with something like:

*   structured
*   assertive (but not confrontational)
*   grounded in reality
*   technically credible
*   business-aligned

* * *

### Your task

#### How do you respond in that meeting that :

*   aligns all stakeholders
*   sets expectations correctly
*   shows leadership
*   avoids future chaos

#### This is where your architect mindset starts forming. In that room, your job is NOT to:

*   give technical details
*   prove you know cloud
*   satisfy everyone individually

Your job is to: **Bring alignment and reduce future conflict**

---

### When multiple stakeholders conflict, use this simple structure


#### 1\. Acknowledge all perspectives

→ shows awareness

#### 2\. Introduce reality/trade-offs

→ shows maturity

#### 3\. Define a structured path forward

→ shows leadership

---
## Sample Answer:

- **What I’m hearing** is three clear priorities — speed of adoption, cost expectations, and strict compliance requirements.
- **We need to be careful** not to optimize for one at the cost of the others, especially in a regulated banking environment.
- My recommendation is that we take a **phased approach** — first establishing a **secure and compliant cloud foundation**, while running a parallel assessment to identify **quick-win** workloads for early migration.
- This allows us to demonstrate progress and controlled cost visibility, without introducing operational or regulatory risk.
- We will also define clear **success metrics** upfront — so we can track cost, risk posture, and adoption in a balanced way.

### Disecting this Answer


#### ✅ “What I’m hearing…”

> You aligned everyone without taking sides.

#### ✅ “We need to be careful…”

> You introduced **trade-offs** (this is senior thinking).

#### ✅ “Phased approach…”

> You gave **direction**, not confusion.

#### ✅ “Secure foundation first…”

> You satisfied the CISO **without saying no to CIO**.

#### ✅ “Quick wins…”

> You satisfied the CFO and CIO **without committing unrealistic timelines**.

#### ✅ “Success metrics…”

> You brought governance and control → leadership signal.

### Selection of Words (Same meaning-Different impact)

Examples:
#### Replace this:

*   “application assessment”
*   “migration wave planning”
*   “reserved instances”

#### With this:

*   “early optimization”
*   “phased savings”
*   “structured cost control”

## Next Scenario

---

### CISO jumps in 

> “I’m not comfortable moving workloads until we are 100% sure cloud meets our regulatory and security requirements.  
> I would prefer we delay migration until full validation is complete.”
---

## This is a trap situation

### If you say

*   “Yes” → program slows down → CIO unhappy
*   “No” → risk exposure → CISO blocks you

### Goal

*   Do NOT reject the CISO
*   Do NOT delay the program
*   Show control, not speed
*   Keep both sides aligned



## Sample Answer

- That’s a valid concern, especially given the regulatory environment we operate in.
- **We will not move forward without** establishing a secure and compliant cloud foundation upfront — including defined policies, guardrails, and **control validation.**
- To balance risk and progress, we’ll start with low-risk, non-production workloads to validate our security and compliance posture against clearly defined **acceptance criteria.**
- Only once those controls are proven and signed off will we move regulated or production workloads.
- This allows us to move forward in a controlled manner, without exposing the organization to unmanaged risk.”

### Disecting this Answer

#### ✅ “We will not move forward without…”

> This gives **absolute assurance** to the CISO.

#### ✅ “control validation / acceptance criteria / sign-off”

> You introduced **governance language** → very important at this level.

#### ✅ “balance risk and progress”

> This is the **core of transformation leadership**

#### ✅ Shorter, sharper, more confident

> Less explanation → more authority

## 🔥 Next Scenario — Internal Conflict (Very Real)

---

### Head of Infrastructure says:

- Why are we even rushing to cloud?
- Our on-prem environment can be optimized at a fraction of the cost.
- We’ve already invested heavily in our data centers.

This is very common resistance. This is not technical. 

> This is political + financial + emotional.

---
### Your challenge

- Do NOT dismiss him
- Do NOT undermine existing investments
- Do NOT slow down the transformation

Show that cloud is not replacing, but evolving strategy

#### You need to sound like

> “I respect your point — and here’s how it fits into the larger strategy without derailing it.”

#### Do not Say:

👉 “We’ll slow down if infra pushes back” 

> You need to absorb the concern, not bend the strategy.

👉 “Cloud is not just infra replacement — it’s a capability shift.” 

> That’s the core argument.

---
## Sample Answer:

- “That’s a fair point — and we absolutely need to maximize the value of our existing data center investments.
- The intent here is not to replace on-prem blindly, but to evolve towards a hybrid model where each workload runs where it makes the most sense.
- We’ll continue to leverage on-prem for stable, long-lived workloads where it remains cost-effective, while using cloud for scenarios where we need scalability, resilience, or faster time-to-delivery that current infrastructure cannot provide.
- This allows us to protect existing investments, while gradually building capabilities that the business will need going forward — rather than delaying that shift entirely.”

### Why this works

✅ “maximize existing investments”

> Infra head feels respected

✅ “not replace blindly”

> Removes threat perception

✅ “hybrid model with workload fit”
> Shows strategy, not ideology

✅ “protect + evolve”
> This is the money line

✅ “rather than delaying that shift”
> You subtly keep momentum

## Key mindset shift:

In these conversations:

- You are not defending cloud
- You are not defending on-prem
- You are *Designing a transition strategy that balances past investment and future capability*

## 🚧 Next Scenario — Strategy Decision

---

### CIO asks you directly:

“Before we go deeper — I want clarity. 

- What is our high-level cloud strategy? 

   - Are we going:

      - Cloud-first
      - Cloud-only
      - Hybrid-first
      - Multi-cloud

  - And why?”

This is a defining moment. Your answer will shape:

- architecture
- investments
- governance
- vendor strategy (vendor independence)
- future multi-cloud (AWS/GCP)
- exit strategy
- long-term positioning

---

### Your recommended strategy

- why (business + technical reasoning)
- how it fits this bank’s reality
- Instead of giving 
  - "“analysis of options” 
  - you must give a “a clear strategic decision”

> Remember : At leadership level, clarity > completeness

---

## Sample Answer:

- “Our strategy will be hybrid-first, evolving towards a controlled multi-cloud model.
- Given the regulatory and operational complexity of a banking environment, a full cloud-only approach is neither practical nor necessary.
- We will take a workload-driven approach — retaining stable and regulated workloads on-prem where it makes sense, while prioritizing cloud adoption for applications that require scalability, resilience, and faster delivery.
- Azure will serve as our primary cloud platform initially, with the architecture designed to support future AWS adoption without creating vendor lock-in.
- This approach allows us to balance compliance, cost, and innovation, while keeping flexibility for future expansion and exit scenarios.”

### Why this works

✅ “Hybrid-first, evolving to multi-cloud”

> You gave a clear, directional strategy

✅ “not practical nor necessary”
> You showed maturity and realism

✅ “workload-driven 

> but after declaring strategy

✅ “Azure now, AWS later, no lock-in”

> You addressed future + exit strategy

✅ “balance compliance, cost, innovation”

> This ties back to earlier stakeholder alignment

---
## Remember

When answering leadership questions, always structure like this:

- Name the strategy (1 line)
- Justify it (2–3 lines)
- Explain how it will be executed (2–3 lines)
