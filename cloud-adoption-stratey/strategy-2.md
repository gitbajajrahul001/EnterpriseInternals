# Strategy Phase – Consulting Approach

The Strategy phase in real-world engagements is less about frameworks and more about **bringing clarity to ambiguity**. 

In most cases, organizations do not come with fully defined answers. The role of a consultant is to structure conversations, uncover gaps, and translate business intent into actionable direction.

---

## 🔹 When Does the Strategy Phase Begin?

The Strategy phase is not confined to a single stage of engagement. It evolves across the lifecycle of a cloud adoption initiative, gaining clarity and depth over time.

---

### 🔸 Pre-RFP (Internal or Early Thinking)

In some organizations, an initial strategy may already exist, typically defined by:

- Enterprise architecture teams  
- Internal strategy groups  
- Previous consulting engagements  

However, this is often **partial, high-level, or unvalidated**.

---

### 🔸 RFP / Pre-Sales Phase

During the RFP stage, consulting partners begin to **shape the strategy** through:

- High-level discovery discussions  
- Understanding business drivers and constraints  
- Interpreting requirements and proposing direction  

> In practice, the RFP process is not just evaluation — it is often where strategy begins to take form.

---

### 🔸 Post-Deal (Discovery & Design Phase)

The Strategy phase is **formally executed after the deal is signed**, typically during:

- Discovery workshops  
- Due diligence (DD) sessions  
- Design discussions  

This is where:

- Assumptions made during RFP are validated  
- Gaps and inconsistencies are identified  
- Business intent is translated into structured direction  

---

### 🔁 Strategy as a Progressive Refinement

Strategy should be viewed as an iterative process:

Initial Idea → Shaped during RFP → Validated in Discovery → Refined during Execution

---

### 🔸 Real-World Consideration

What is defined during the RFP stage is often:

- Incomplete  
- Based on assumptions  
- Subject to change after deeper analysis  

For example:

- An initial assumption to migrate all workloads may evolve after discovery reveals dependencies, constraints, or technical limitations.

---

### 🧠 Key Insight

> Strategy is not created once — it is continuously refined as clarity improves.

---

## 🧭 Approach to Strategy Discovery

A structured approach is essential to avoid fragmented understanding. Instead of asking isolated questions, discussions should be guided across key themes. Each layer builds on the previous one, forming a complete picture of the enterprise.

### 🔷 1. Business & Objectives

Focus on understanding *why* the organization is considering cloud adoption.

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|-------------|
| Drivers | What are the primary business drivers for cloud adoption? |
| Outcomes | What does success look like in the next 6–12 months? |
| Priorities | Are there specific timelines or mandates driving this initiative? |
| Impact | What happens if this initiative does not succeed? |

> In practice, business goals are often broad or undefined, requiring refinement through discussion.

---

### 🔷 2. Application Landscape

Understanding the current estate is critical before any decision is made.

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|-------------|
| Inventory | Do you have a complete list of applications and workloads? |
| Criticality | Which applications are business-critical? |
| Dependencies | Are inter-application dependencies documented? |
| Stability | Which applications have historically caused outages? |

> Documentation is often incomplete; real insights come from discussions and operational history.

---

### 🔷 3. Compliance & Constraints

This layer defines what is non-negotiable.

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|-------------|
| Regulations | What regulatory frameworks apply (e.g., RBI, GDPR, HIPAA)? |
| Data | Are there data residency or sovereignty requirements? |
| Restrictions | Are there workloads that cannot move to cloud? |
| Audits | Are there audit or certification requirements? |

> These constraints directly shape architectural decisions and must be identified early.

---

### 🔷 4. Risk, Resilience & Continuity

This determines how robust the architecture needs to be.

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|-------------|
| Availability | What level of availability is required? |
| Recovery | What are the expected RTO and RPO targets? |
| DR | Is disaster recovery tested regularly? |

> Many organizations define recovery expectations but do not validate them through testing.

---

### 🔷 5. Organization & Operating Model

Cloud adoption success depends on who operates it.

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|-------------|
| Structure | How are teams currently organized? |
| Ownership | Who owns infrastructure, security, and applications? |
| Skills | What cloud or automation skills exist internally? |
| Operations | Who will operate the cloud environment post-deployment? |

> Misalignment between strategy and organizational readiness is a common failure point.

---

### 🔷 6. Governance & Cost

Without early governance, cloud environments become difficult to control.

| Area | Key Questions |
|------|-------------|
| Access | How are permissions and access managed today? |
| Cost | How is IT spend tracked and controlled? |
| Accountability | Who is responsible for cloud cost? |
| Standards | Are there tagging or governance standards in place? |

> Governance is not about restriction — it is about enabling controlled scalability.

---

## ⚠️ Handling Gaps and Unclear Responses

In real engagements, answers are often incomplete, conflicting, or unavailable.

### Common Scenarios

| Situation | Approach |
|----------|---------|
| Missing answers | Provide structured options and guide decision-making |
| Conflicting inputs | Highlight gaps and align stakeholders |
| Everything is critical | Prioritize based on business impact |
| No documentation | Use workshops, interviews, and operational data |

> The objective is not to extract perfect answers, but to move the organization toward structured clarity.

---

## 📄 Outcome of Strategy Discovery

At the end of this phase, the output should include:

- Business drivers and success criteria  
- Application landscape overview  
- Compliance and regulatory constraints  
- Risk profile (RTO/RPO expectations)  
- Organizational readiness assessment  
- Identified gaps and risks  

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

> The value of a consultant lies not in having predefined answers, but in asking the right questions and structuring ambiguity into clear direction.

---

## 📊 Consolidated Question Set

The following table provides a consolidated and structured set of questions across all categories:

| Category | Key Questions |
|----------|--------------|
| Business & Objectives | What are the primary drivers for cloud adoption? What outcomes define success? What are the timelines or mandates? What are the consequences of failure? |
| Application Landscape | Do you have a complete application inventory? Which workloads are business-critical? What dependencies exist? Which systems are unstable or failure-prone? |
| Compliance & Constraints | What regulations apply? Are there data residency requirements? Are any workloads restricted from cloud? What audit or certification requirements exist? |
| Risk & Continuity | What are the RTO and RPO targets? What level of availability is required? Are backups and DR tested regularly? What is the business impact of downtime? |
| Organization & Operating Model | How are teams structured? Who owns operations post-migration? What skills exist internally? Are DevOps practices in place? |
| Governance & Cost | How is access controlled? How is cost tracked and optimized? Who is accountable for spend? Are governance standards (tagging, policies) defined? |

## 🧪 Strategy Discovery Workshop (How This Is Executed)

While the above sections outline the questions and approach, in practice these are executed through structured workshops with stakeholders. The Strategy phase is typically executed through structured workshops with key stakeholders. The goal is to move from **high-level intent to structured clarity**, as this is typically how the Strategy phase is executed in real client engagements.


---

### 🧭 Workshop Objectives

- Understand business drivers for cloud adoption  
- Identify application landscape and dependencies  
- Capture compliance and regulatory constraints  
- Define risk, resilience, and continuity expectations  
- Assess organizational readiness  
- Highlight gaps and risks  

---

### 👥 Participants

| Role | Purpose |
|------|--------|
| Business Stakeholders | Define goals, priorities, and success criteria |
| IT Leadership | Provide current-state architecture and constraints |
| Application Owners | Share workload details and dependencies |
| Security/Compliance Teams | Define regulatory and risk requirements |
| Operations Teams | Provide insights into current processes and challenges |

---

### ⏱️ Suggested Duration & Flow

- Total: **2–4 hours**
- Can be split into multiple focused sessions if required

#### 🔷 1. Introduction & Context Setting (15–20 min)

**Objective:** Align stakeholders on purpose and expectations

#### 🔷 2. Business Drivers & Objectives (30–40 min)

**Objective:** Understand *why* cloud adoption is being considered


#### 🔷 3. Application Landscape Discovery (40–50 min)

**Objective:** Understand *what* is being moved

### 🔷 4. Compliance & Constraints (30–40 min)

**Objective:** Identify non-negotiable requirements


#### 🔷 5. Risk, Resilience & Continuity (30–40 min)

**Objective:** Define availability and recovery expectations


#### 🔷 6. Organization & Operating Model (30–40 min)

**Objective:** Understand *who* will operate the cloud


#### 🔷 7. Governance & Cost (20–30 min)

**Objective:** Establish control mechanisms early


#### 🔷 8. Gap Identification & Alignment (20–30 min)

**Objective:** Highlight inconsistencies and missing information


#### 📄 **Workshop Deliverables**

After the workshop, the following should be documented:

- Business drivers and success criteria  
- Application landscape overview  
- Compliance and regulatory constraints  
- Risk profile (RTO/RPO expectations)  
- Organizational readiness assessment  
- Governance and cost insights  
- Identified gaps and risks  

---

### ⚠️ Key Considerations

- Avoid deep technical dives in initial sessions  
- Focus on structured discussions, not perfect answers  
- Validate assumptions with multiple stakeholders  
- Document gaps clearly — they are as important as answers  

---

### 🧠 Consulting Insight

> A successful strategy workshop does not aim to collect perfect information, but to transform unclear inputs into structured direction.
