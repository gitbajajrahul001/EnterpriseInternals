# ACME Corp – Cloud Transformation Case Study

## 🧭 Overview

ACME Corp is a large multinational banking organization operating across multiple regions, including North America, Europe, and Asia.

The organization manages:
- Core banking platforms  
- Payment processing systems  
- Customer-facing digital applications  
- Regulatory reporting systems  

Their technology landscape has evolved over time and includes:
- Legacy monolithic applications running on on-prem data centers  
- Multiple regional data centers with inconsistent standards  
- Limited automation and heavy reliance on manual operations  

---

## 🔍 Current State Challenges

ACME Corp initiated its cloud journey with the expectation of improving agility and reducing infrastructure overhead. However, several challenges were identified:

### **1. Fragmented Application Landscape**

- No centralized application inventory  
- Unknown dependencies between systems  
- Region-specific implementations of similar workloads  

---

### **2. Regulatory & Compliance Complexity**

- Data residency requirements across regions  (e.g., EU workloads restricted under GDPR, India workloads under RBI guidelines)
- Strict audit and reporting requirements  
- Sensitive financial data requiring enhanced controls  

---

### **3. Operational Inefficiencies**

- Manual provisioning of infrastructure  
- Long lead times for environment setup  (e.g., weeks to provision new environments)
- Inconsistent deployment processes across teams  

---

### **4. Lack of Standardization**

- Different teams following different architecture patterns  
- No unified governance or policy enforcement  
- Security controls implemented inconsistently  

---

### **5. Business Pressure**

- Demand for faster digital product releases  
- Need to integrate with fintech partners  
- Increasing competition from cloud-native banks  

---

## 🎯 Initial Objective (Client Perspective)

ACME Corp defined its initial objective as **Migrate all workloads to cloud to improve agility and reduce cost**

---

## ⚠️ Initial Observation (Consulting Perspective)

At the start of the engagement, this objective appeared:

- overly broad  
- not aligned with application realities  
- lacking prioritization  

---

## 🧠 Key Insight

The problem was not cloud adoption — it was lack of clarity on **what should move, why it should move, and how it should be executed**

---

## 🔗 What Comes Next

The engagement begins with the **Strategy Phase**, where initial assumptions are validated, refined, and structured into a clear direction.
